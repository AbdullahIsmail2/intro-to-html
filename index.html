Mount Everest

The Roof of the World

Key facts
  • Height: 8,848 meters (29,029 feet)
  • Location: Border of Nepal and China (Tibet)
  • First Ascent: Edmund Hillary and Tenzing Norgay in 1953

Mount Everest, towering at a staggering 8,848 meters (29,029 feet) above sea level, is not only the highest peak on Earth but also a symbol of human determination and endurance. Nestled in the majestic Himalayas, on the border between Nepal and the Tibet Autonomous Region of China, Everest has captivated adventurers and climbers for generations. The history of its ascents is filled with tales of triumph and tragedy, from the first successful climb by Sir Edmund Hillary and Tenzing Norgay in 1953 to the countless stories of those who have since sought to conquer its icy slopes. Climbers face extreme conditions, including sub-zero temperatures, fierce winds, and the notorious "Death Zone" above 8,000 meters, where the lack of oxygen makes each step a monumental effort. Despite these challenges, the allure of standing atop the world's highest point continues to draw people from around the globe, making Everest a timeless beacon of exploration and human spirit.
